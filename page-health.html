Available at: https://labblog.uofmhealth.org/lab-report/scientists-train-computers-to-recognize-which-early-stage-breast-cancers-will-spread [Accessed 3 Nov. 2021].
ü¶† Currently around 1 in 5 breast cancers are caught in the early stages, before they spread into the surrounding tissue. However, it cannot be predicted which of these "stage O" cancers are likely to spread after surgery, and which are likely to be cured. This has all changed thanks to research by the U-M Rogel Cancer Center which has developed a new diagnostic approach using artificial intelligence to determine with over 90% accuracy, which cancers are likely to spread after surgery. The computer looks for patterns that a human would not be able to detect in high-resolution images taken of patient tissue in order to come to a conclusion. With breast cancer affecting 1 in 7 UK females (https://www.cancerresearchuk.org/health-professional/cancer-statistics/statistics-by-cancer-type/breast-cancer), this breakthrough will allow patients and doctors to decide on the best course of treatment, and will hopefully spare women from unnecessary mastectomies.
